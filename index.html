About

We build carefully, hold what we build, and run it ourselves.

In 1936, a fifteen-year-old named Clarence Harvey Byler moved with his family from Burton, Ohio to the farmland of Princess Anne County, the place that would become Virginia Beach. After serving in the Air Force he started building houses, and by the 1960s "Curly" Byler had become one of the city's leading residential developers. He created the Larkspur neighborhood in Kempsville, hundreds of homes on treed lots, and named its main road for his friend and attorney Edwin Kellam. In January 1964, with Larkspur in full stride, he incorporated Tidewater Investment Co. to hold what he was building.

He held what he built and ran it himself. The family has kept that philosophy for sixty years and three generations, from those first homes to the commercial properties the company still owns and operates today.

Today the company is led by his daughter, Kathryn N. Byler, and his grandson, Patrick Byler Clark. We remain what we have always been: private, family-owned, and directly involved in every property we hold. We answer our own phone, we know our tenants by name, and we make decisions with the next generation in mind rather than the next quarter.

Built to be kept, not sold. Held for the long term, and operated to a family standard.
